User Guide

Getting Started
Set up a company first. The company controls your pay rules, overtime logic, leave allowance, pay-cycle timing, default times, and whether vehicle, trailer, and mileage fields appear on the shift form.
1. Add your company
Open Companies and create your main employer or agency. If you are paid by an agency but work for different client sites, set the agency as your default company because the default company controls 4-week block timing and other company-based defaults.
2. Configure pay and shift rules
In the company record, set base rate, overtime mode, overtime scheme, overtime multipliers, pay cycle, base weekly hours or daily OT threshold, minimum paid shift hours, leave allowance, standard shift length, fixed or variable break deductions, bonus rules, and night-out pay.
3. Enter shifts
Use Enter Shift to add worked shifts, annual leave, sick days, vehicles, trailers, mileage, defects, expenses, overrides, and night-out pay. New vehicle registrations typed on the shift form are saved automatically and assigned to the selected company.
4. Manage vehicles when needed
The Vehicles page is the master list for all saved registrations. Use it to review or delete vehicles overall. Company assignment is controlled from Companies and also happens automatically when you save a shift with a typed registration.
5. Review pay and history
Home gives a quick current-period view, Summary shows full weekly and pay-period totals, and Shift Calendar lets you add or edit shifts by date.

Page Guide

Home
Shows at-a-glance totals for This Week and your current pay period. If the default company is 4-weekly, the second section becomes the current 4-week block and shows its start and end dates. The annual leave and sickness section shows yearly totals, not monthly totals.
Enter Shift
Choose the company first so the form can apply that company’s rules and defaults. Vehicles can be added one row at a time with Add Vehicle. Trailers work the same way with Add Trailer. If mileage fields are enabled for that company, each vehicle row can store start mileage, finish mileage, and the calculated total. Annual leave and sick day are mutually exclusive. Day off in lieu only applies to worked shifts. Night out, parking, tolls, and company-specific overrides are all stored on the shift.
How Pay Is Calculated
Weekly overtime companies use base weekly hours before overtime begins. Daily overtime companies use a per-shift worked-hours threshold. The overtime scheme decides how overtime is priced: by day type, at one flat overtime rate, by worked-day sequence, or with no overtime premium. In the worked-day sequence scheme, unique worked dates are counted Monday to Sunday so days 1-5 can use one overtime rule, day 6 another, and day 7 or bank holiday another. Base rate comes from the company unless you set a shift override. Breaks can be a fixed deduction or variable rules based on hours worked. Bonus rules can pay per hour in a time window, per shift, per week, or as a flat per-shift bonus. Night-out pay is added on top. Expenses are tracked separately and shown in summaries and exports.
Companies
Stores all company-level logic: pay mode, pay cycle, 4-week cycle start date, leave allowance, default start and finish times, standard shift length, overtime multipliers, bonus rules, minimum paid shift hours, night-out pay, field visibility, contact details, and assigned vehicles. Only vehicles assigned to that company appear in the Enter Shift suggestions.
Vehicles
Shows the full registration list across the app. Each vehicle now shows the companies it is currently assigned to. Delete with care because removing a vehicle also removes its assignment from company records.
Summary
The top toggle shows Week and either Month or Current 4-Week Block depending on the default company pay cycle. The current 4-week block shows its start and end dates. Summary includes overall totals, company-by-company breakdowns, leave and sickness totals, and export buttons for PDF or simple export.
Shift Calendar
Lets you browse shifts by month, click a date, add a worked shift for that date, or quickly add annual leave or a sick day. Existing shifts on the selected date can be edited from their shift cards.
Settings
Settings contains update checks and backup/restore. Backup saves your companies, shifts, vehicles, and settings. Restore loads a previous backup and migrates older data formats automatically where possible.

Shift Detail Reference

Worked Shift Fields
Date, start time, finish time, shift type, vehicles, trailers, mileage, defects/notes, bank holiday flag, day off in lieu, night out, expenses, and company overrides can all affect what appears later in Summary and exports.
Vehicle and Mileage Logic
Vehicle rows are stored per shift. A row can contain just a registration, or a registration plus mileage data. The shift display shows mileage as start, finish, and total. Vehicles typed during entry are converted to uppercase, saved globally, and assigned to the selected company automatically.
Leave and Sickness
Annual leave and sick days use the company standard shift length and break rules to derive paid hours. Yearly leave balance is calculated as total annual leave allowance across selectable companies, plus lieu days earned, minus annual leave taken.

FAQ & Troubleshooting

Why is the wrong pay period showing?
Check which company is set as your default company and confirm that company’s pay cycle. A 4-weekly default company changes Summary and Home to the current 4-week block.
Why is overtime wrong?
Check the company pay mode first. Weekly overtime depends on base weekly hours. Daily overtime depends on the daily OT threshold or standard shift length fallback. Also check the overtime scheme, the overtime multipliers attached to that scheme, and any shift-level overrides.
Why are vehicles or mileage fields missing?
Open the company and check the Shift Entry Fields section. Vehicle, trailer, and mileage fields can be turned on or off per company.
Why can’t I see a vehicle in the Enter Shift suggestions?
The vehicle may not be assigned to that company yet. Assign it in Companies, or type the registration directly on the shift form and save the shift to assign it automatically.
Why does leave balance look wrong?
Check each company’s annual leave allowance, make sure annual leave was logged as leave rather than a worked shift, and confirm any lieu days were recorded with the day off in lieu option.
Why is a leave or sick day paid at the wrong hours?
Check the company standard shift length and break setup. Leave and sick pay use that standard length minus the company break deduction.
Why are exports or totals different from what I expected?
Review company base rate, overtime multipliers, bonus rules, minimum paid shift hours, night-out pay, and any per-shift overrides. Most calculation issues come from company setup rather than the shift itself.
